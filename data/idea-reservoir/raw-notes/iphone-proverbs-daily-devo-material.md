Proverbs 25:2 and the slowed light fact:

“It is the glory of God to conceal a matter; to search out a matter is the glory of kings.” (Proverbs 25:2)

Think about this: in 2008, researchers figured out how to slow down light—yes, light itself. What travels at an unthinkable speed in a vacuum could be slowed to just a few miles per hour by passing it through a special medium. It’s like they uncovered a hidden law of creation, a glimpse of God’s glory woven into the universe.

Proverbs reminds us that God hides mysteries in His creation—not to keep us in the dark, but to invite us to seek, to explore, and to marvel. Whether it’s the speed of light or the depths of wisdom, there’s glory in uncovering what He’s concealed. So today, ask yourself: what mystery is God inviting you to search out? Because when we seek with diligence, we reflect His glory.
