# Idea Reservoir Strategy

The Idea Reservoir stores creative seeds for future Proverbs Daily episodes.

Ideas should not be treated as published content. They are unused creative inventory: story ideas, title ideas, quotes, podcast references, cultural hooks, sermons, conversations, news observations, personal stories, field notes, and future episode directions Fred may want to return to later.

Obsidian Seeds are the primary Idea Reservoir input. Fred's full Obsidian vault, FlynchMind, remains the master second brain and should not be committed into this repo. Only curated Proverbs Daily relevant markdown files should be copied or synced into `data/idea-reservoir/obsidian-seeds/`.

iPhone Notes are secondary raw legacy input. They may still be useful, but new idea capture should favor the curated Obsidian Seeds workflow.

Keep Obsidian markdown links, tags, and backlinks when present. They may help future matching, source tracing, and dashboard suggestions.

Ideas may be linked to possible verses, chapters, personas, themes, or source references. These links are suggestive only. A possible verse match does not mean the idea has been approved for that scripture.

Unused ideas remain available. Rejected ideas are not deleted, because rejected ideas may still carry useful context, warnings, or future inspiration.

Used ideas should be marked with the episode, date, and scripture reference where they were used. This allows the dashboard to avoid repeatedly recommending the same creative seed while still preserving the original idea record.

Ideas can come from iPhone notes, podcasts, books, quotes, sermons, conversations, news, personal stories, or field observations.

The dashboard should eventually suggest 1-3 possible episode directions per day using unused verses plus available ideas.
