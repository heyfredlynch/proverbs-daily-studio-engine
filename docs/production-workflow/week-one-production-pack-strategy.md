# Season Two Week One Production Pack Strategy

## Source of Truth
The FINAL docx is the production source of truth: data/season-two-exemplars/FINAL-PD-Script Generator Refinement Project June 7-13.docx. Older rough draft files are not production sources and should only be consulted for reference when absolutely necessary.

## Pack Structure
- Approved master scripts live in `episodes/approved/week-one/` and are for production review.
- Substack files live in `outputs/reader/substack/week-one/` and are reader-facing.
- ProverbsDaily.org files live in `outputs/reader/proverbsdaily-org/week-one/` and are structured for app/web publishing.
- ElevenLabs files live in `outputs/performance/elevenlabs/week-one/` and are audio-safe plain spoken text.
- Video planning files live in `outputs/video/planning/week-one/` and are creative production notes.

## Internal Notes
Recommended Improvements and Fred Revision Notes are internal only. They may guide video planning or production decisions, but they should not appear in reader-facing, app/web, or ElevenLabs files.

## v0.2 Formatting Note
The FINAL document opening sequence is authoritative: scripture reference, verse text, then `Today’s Episode: [title]`. Codex must not smooth, rephrase, or add generated opening language such as `Today’s Proverbs Daily is called...`, `Proverbs chapter seven, verse one says...`, or similar introductions.

Outro bumper rules are platform-specific: Substack keeps the full reader-facing bumper with emojis; ElevenLabs keeps the full spoken bumper with emojis removed only; approved master scripts keep the bumper as part of the approved master; video planning files reference the bumper as the closing/CTA sequence.

ProverbsDaily.org excludes the outro bumper because the web app already has its own About the Author card. Those files should end after Today's Challenge.

## Editorial Authority
Fred remains the final editorial authority for script changes, performance choices, publishing decisions, and any unresolved extraction questions.
