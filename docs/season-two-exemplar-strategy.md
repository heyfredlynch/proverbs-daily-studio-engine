# Season Two Exemplar Strategy

The June 7-13 refinement document becomes the first official Season Two Style Exemplar.

Primary source input:

`data/season-two-exemplars/PD-Script Generator Refinement Project June 7-13.docx`

This document captures Fred's live producer dialogue with ChatGPT and the refined Season Two script development process. It should become the first source for understanding the current Season Two direction.

## Exemplar Role

The scripts developed in the June 7-13 refinement document represent the current standard for Season Two direction.

They should help future generation work understand:

- how Season Two differs from Season One
- how Fred frames titles and episode instincts
- how theological direction is refined in conversation
- how rough AI drafts are corrected
- how final scripts become more like Fred's voice

## What The Engine Should Learn From

When this exemplar source is later reviewed or encoded, the engine should learn from:

- Fred's prompts
- AI drafts
- Fred's corrections
- final accepted scripts

The learning target is not only the finished text. The difference between the AI draft and Fred's correction is one of the most valuable signals.

## Raw Dialogue And Approved Scripts

Final Fred-approved scripts should be stored separately from raw dialogue.

The raw dialogue preserves process, judgment, and revision history. The approved scripts preserve the production standard. Both are valuable, but they should not be collapsed into one source.

Future storage should distinguish:

- raw producer-session dialogue
- rough AI-generated drafts
- Fred revision notes
- final Fred-approved scripts
- publication-ready reader or performance versions

## Editorial Goal

The goal is to capture the difference between AI draft and Fred voice.

Future generation should become better at hearing:

- where a draft sounds generic
- where a title needs sharper fit
- where the theology needs more precision
- where the hook needs more lived tension
- where the application needs to become more practical
- where the voice needs to become warmer, clearer, or more direct

The exemplar layer should help the engine become a better creative partner inside Fred's process, not a replacement for Fred's editorial judgment.

## Boundaries

Do not parse the `.docx` source yet.

Do not modify existing engines yet.

Do not merge raw dialogue and final approved scripts into a single undifferentiated training source.

Future work may define:

- exemplar extraction rules
- accepted script storage paths
- revision comparison notes
- prompt refinement rules
- voice calibration examples
