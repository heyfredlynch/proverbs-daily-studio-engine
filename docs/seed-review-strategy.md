# Seed Review Strategy

Fred's monthly seed review process exists to let ideas breathe before they are forced into structure.

## Principles

1. Seeds are captured before they are understood.
2. Seeds may remain unassigned for months or years.
3. The goal is not immediate categorization.
4. Review sessions look for:
   - new connections
   - recurring themes
   - matching Proverbs verses
   - cultural relevance
   - personal relevance
5. Seeds may move through states:
   - Captured
   - Incubating
   - Reviewing
   - Suggested
   - Selected
   - Used
   - Dormant
   - Archived
6. Dormant is not failure. Dormant means waiting for season and context.
7. The Idea Reservoir should preserve uncertainty.

## Operational State vs Creative Lifecycle State

`status` describes the operational state of an idea in the production system.

`reviewStatus` describes the creative lifecycle state of an idea as Fred revisits it over time.

An idea may be operationally `Available` while creatively `Incubating`. That distinction protects unfinished ideas from being prematurely rejected, over-labeled, or forced into the wrong episode.
