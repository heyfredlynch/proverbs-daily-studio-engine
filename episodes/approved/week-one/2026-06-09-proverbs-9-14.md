## Reader Version

# Watch Who’s Whispering

**Proverbs 9:14 (TPT)**

“And there she sits at the gateway to the high places, on her throne overlooking the city.”

Do you remember the infamous slap seen around the world?

The 2022 Academy Awards.

Will Smith walks on stage and slaps Chris Rock in front of millions of viewers.

Now here’s what’s fascinating.

Will Smith wasn’t some unknown person struggling to make it.

He was at the top.

Successful. Respected. Award-winning.

In fact, he was only moments away from receiving one of the highest honors of his career.

Yet in a single moment, one thought, one impulse, one decision changed the entire conversation.

How does that happen?

How can someone who has accomplished so much suddenly do something so foolish?

Proverbs gives us an answer:

This verse is talking about Folly.

The foolish voice.

The destructive voice.

And notice where she is sitting.

At the gateway.

In the high places.

Overlooking the city.

In other words, Folly isn’t hiding in some dark alley.

She’s positioned right where people enter.

Right where decisions are made.

Right where influence is gained.

Just like Wisdom stands at the crossroads calling out to us, Folly sits nearby offering her own suggestions.

Her timing is strategic.

She doesn’t wait until you’re at your worst.

She often shows up when you’re at your best.

When you’re successful. When you’re celebrated. When you’re finally getting what you’ve worked for.

That’s when she leans in and whispers.

Human beings have an incredible capacity for good.

We create art. Build families. Care for others. Change lives.

But we also have the capacity to make spectacularly bad decisions.

Not because we’re evil.

Because we listen to the wrong voice.

One foolish thought can feel reasonable in the moment.

One impulsive reaction can seem justified.

One emotional response can masquerade as wisdom.

That’s why Proverbs warns us to pay attention to who’s talking inside our heads.

After the Oscars incident, Denzel Washington shared something with Will Smith that has been repeated many times since.

He said:

“At your highest moment, be careful. That’s when the devil comes for you.”

Whether you agree with every detail of that statement or not, the principle is powerful.

When you’re on top, don’t assume you’re immune.

When you’re winning, don’t assume every thought deserves your trust.

When you’re emotional, don’t assume every impulse deserves your obedience.

The good news is that wisdom is speaking too.

You don’t have to follow every voice that enters your mind.

You can pause.

You can examine it.

You can choose differently.
