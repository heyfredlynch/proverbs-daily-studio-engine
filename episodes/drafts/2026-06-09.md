## Working Episode Title

Wisdom Invites The Unfinished

## Hook

There are rooms we do not enter because we think we should already know enough to belong there.

We tell ourselves, "I am not ready."

Sometimes that is humility. Sometimes it is fear wearing a responsible outfit.

## Devotional Body

Proverbs 9:4 gives us a picture of wisdom inviting the simple, the unfinished, the ones who still need formation. Wisdom is not embarrassed by beginners.

That is good news.

Because most of us are more unfinished than we admit. We know enough to look competent in certain rooms, but there are still places in us that need teaching. Places where we react before we reflect. Places where we defend before we listen. Places where we confuse being experienced with being wise.

Wisdom does not wait until we are polished to call us.

Wisdom invites us while we are still learning.

That means the invitation is not an insult. When wisdom says, "Come here," it is not shaming you for what you do not know. It is opening a door to what you can become.

A father understands this tension. You want your children to grow, but you do not want them to think love is waiting on the other side of mastery. You invite them into learning because you already love them, not because they have already figured everything out.

God's wisdom works that way too.

It calls to the unformed places in us, not to condemn them, but to disciple them.

The proud person hears an invitation and feels attacked. The teachable person hears an invitation and feels hope.

That may be the real test. Not whether you know everything. Not whether you have outgrown correction. But whether you can still receive an invitation to learn without needing to protect your image.

Sometimes the most spiritual thing you can say is, "I do not know yet."

That sentence can feel small, but it opens a large door. It lets a mentor speak. It lets a child ask another question. It lets God meet you somewhere other than your performance. It gives wisdom room to do more than confirm what you already thought.

There is a kind of maturity that looks like certainty. But there is a deeper maturity that can stay curious without falling apart.

A father does not need to have every answer to be faithful. A disciple does not need to master every lesson before entering the room. Wisdom is not calling the finished. Wisdom is calling the willing.

Wisdom has a table. The question is whether we are humble enough to sit down.

Today, do not despise the place where you are still a student. That may be the very place wisdom is calling your name.

## Remember

Wisdom does not shame the unfinished; it invites them.
