## Hook

Some talk feels cheap because it is easy to spend.

A comment. A joke. A post. A little exaggeration. A little heat because the room was boring and somebody wanted a spark.

But cheap speech still sends a bill.

## Devotional Body

Proverbs 10:3 speaks about hunger, desire, and what God does not abandon. It reminds us that appetite is never just physical. We hunger for food, but we also hunger for attention, control, approval, revenge, and the feeling of being right.

And when that hunger gets loud, the mouth often moves first.

That is why speech and desire are connected. We say things because something in us wants to be fed. We want the laugh. We want the win. We want the last word. We want to feel bigger than the person we just made smaller.

But Proverbs keeps dragging us back to wisdom: desire has consequences.

Words are not weightless. They build trust or break it. They heal a room or poison it. They reveal whether our hunger is being governed by wisdom or driven by impulse.

In public life, this matters. In family, it matters even more. The people closest to us often hear the words we would edit before saying anywhere else. We can call it honesty, but sometimes it is just hunger with no discipline.

Sometimes the mouth is not telling the truth. It is auditioning for power.

It wants to win the argument. It wants to control the story. It wants somebody to clap, repost, agree, or back down. And in that moment, the issue is not vocabulary. The issue is appetite.

That is why wisdom has to reach deeper than speech technique. You can learn how to sound calm and still be cruel. You can learn how to sound spiritual and still be manipulative. Proverbs is after the heart that is feeding the sentence.

The Lord is not indifferent to hunger. Proverbs does not tell us to pretend we do not want anything. It shows us that God cares about appetite, provision, righteousness, and the direction desire takes.

So before the mouth spends what the heart cannot afford, wisdom asks a better question.

What am I hungry for right now?

Am I hungry for peace, or for control?

Am I hungry for truth, or for attention?

Am I hungry for justice, or just the pleasure of landing a clean hit?

Cheep speech ain't free. The misspelling almost tells the truth. It chirps fast, makes noise, and flies away. But the people who heard it still have to live with what landed.

Today, let wisdom govern your hunger before your mouth starts spending.
