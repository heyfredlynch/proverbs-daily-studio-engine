## Hook

Most of us will not change the whole world before lunch.

But we can change the weight of a room.

We can make the next interaction more honest, more generous, more human. And sometimes that is where justice starts.

## Devotional Body

Proverbs 11:1 brings wisdom into the marketplace. It speaks about honest measures, about the way people weigh value and treat one another when money, power, and advantage are in the room.

That is not abstract spirituality. That is everyday righteousness.

Wisdom cares about scales because wisdom cares about people. A dishonest scale does not just mismeasure grain. It mismeasures a neighbor. It says, "I can take a little more from you because I can get away with it."

That spirit still exists.

It shows up when we shade the truth to protect our advantage. When we give someone less attention because they cannot help us. When we make the room better for important people and colder for everybody else.

And the uncomfortable part is that most injustice does not begin with someone announcing, "I would like to be unfair today."

It begins with a small adjustment. A little less honesty. A little less empathy. A little more benefit for me, a little less burden for you. The scale tilts quietly before the damage becomes visible.

That is why Proverbs brings holiness down to the counter, the contract, the conversation, the checkout line, the email, the meeting, the moment where nobody may applaud you for doing the right thing.

But Proverbs reminds us that justice begins in the ordinary. In how we measure. How we speak. How we treat the person who cannot repay us. How we handle small opportunities to be fair.

Making the world better is not always dramatic. Sometimes it is refusing to cheat in a small place. Sometimes it is telling the truth when a lie would be convenient. Sometimes it is bringing warmth into a room that has been running on efficiency and suspicion.

The citizen persona matters here. Wisdom is not only private character. It is public atmosphere. A wise person helps the community breathe better.

Maybe today the assignment is not to fix everything.

Maybe it is to make one room more honest.

One conversation more gracious.

One transaction more fair.

One person feel less invisible.

That may not trend. It may not announce itself as world-changing. But goodness has a way of traveling. A just scale in one hand can become trust in another heart.

So yes, make the world a better place.

Start with the room you are in.

## Remember

Justice often begins with the small measure in your own hand.
