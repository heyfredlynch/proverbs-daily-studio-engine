## Hook

Everybody wants to be solid when the storm comes.

But very few people enjoy the correction that makes them solid.

We want the strength without the training, the stability without the feedback, the rock without the pressure that proved what it was.

## Devotional Body

Proverbs 12:1 is about loving discipline and receiving correction. It is not soft language, but it is loving wisdom.

The verse presses on a truth most of us would rather avoid: your relationship with correction reveals your relationship with growth.

Anybody can say they want wisdom. But when wisdom arrives as correction, the room gets quiet. Suddenly we start explaining. Defending. Reframing. Looking for a softer translation of what we just heard.

That is human.

But it is also dangerous.

Because the correction we reject may be the very tool God intended to use to make us steady.

A father learns this from both sides. You give correction and watch it land badly. You receive correction and feel your own pride flare up. You realize discipline is not punishment when love is behind it. Discipline is formation. It is love refusing to let weakness stay hidden until the storm exposes it.

Think about practice. Music practice, workout discipline, clinical training, parenting, marriage. The correction rarely feels poetic in the moment. It feels like repetition. It feels like feedback. It feels like somebody noticing the thing you hoped they would miss.

But later, under pressure, you become grateful for what correction built.

The note is cleaner. The body is stronger. The apology comes faster. The ego does not have to drive every conversation. The foundation holds because somebody loved you enough to tell you the truth before the storm did.

That is why "Like A Rock" cannot just be about being tough. Biblical stability is not stubbornness. Some people call themselves solid when they are really just unteachable.

Wisdom makes a different kind of strength.

It is strong enough to listen.

Strong enough to be corrected.

Strong enough to change before consequences become the teacher.

The storm does not create the foundation. It reveals it. And correction is one of the ways God works on the foundation before the weather changes.

So if someone you trust tells you the truth today, do not rush to protect your image. Pause. Breathe. Ask whether love might be hiding inside the discomfort.

Because the person who loves discipline is not someone who enjoys pain. It is someone who wants wisdom more than ego.

That kind of person becomes steady.

Not loud. Not flashy.

Steady.

Like a rock.
