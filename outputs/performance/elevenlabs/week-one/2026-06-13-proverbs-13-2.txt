Proverbs 13:2

From the fruit of their lips people enjoy good things, but the unfaithful have an appetite for violence.

Today’s Episode: Armageddon Appetite

Back in my college days I discovered a band from Chicago called Resurrection Band.

Most people knew them as Rez Band.

They weren’t hip hop.

They were straight-up rock and roll mixed with Chicago blues.

And they had this strange little song called “Armageddon Appetite.”

To this day I still remember it.

Not because it was their biggest song.

But because the title stuck with me.

An appetite for destruction.

An appetite for chaos.

An appetite for the end of things.

And the older I get, the more I realize:

Everybody’s feeding something.

This proverb is built around food.

Fruit.

Appetite.

Consumption.

Taste.

The writer is asking a simple question:

What are you feeding on?

Because eventually what you consume becomes what you crave.

That’s how appetite works.

Nobody wakes up one morning craving something they’ve never tasted.

Appetites are cultivated.

Developed.

Acquired.

What you chew on today shapes what you’ll hunger for tomorrow.

Think about “food for thought.”

We use that phrase all the time.

Because thoughts are a lot like meals.

Some nourish.

Some poison.

Some leave you stronger.

Others leave you restless.

You can spend all day feeding on outrage.

Conflict.

Fear.

Division.

And before long you’ll develop a taste for it.

An acquired taste.

An Armageddon Appetite.

Or you can feed on wisdom.

Hope.

Truth.

Beauty.

Gratitude.

And slowly your soul learns to hunger for those things instead.

The good news is that appetites can change.

You are not trapped by yesterday’s cravings.

Every day gives you a new menu.

A new opportunity to choose what enters your mind and settles in your heart.

What you repeatedly meditate on eventually becomes what you naturally desire.

And what you naturally desire eventually shapes your life.

So choose carefully.

Because your future is being fed right now.

What you feed today becomes what you hunger for tomorrow.

God, give me an appetite for wisdom, truth, and life.

Pay attention to what you’re mentally consuming today.

Ask yourself:

Is this feeding fear, or feeding wisdom?

Then choose one thing that nourishes your soul and spend time chewing on that instead.
