scriptureReference: Proverbs 11:1
translation: NIV
verseText: |-
  “The Lord detests dishonest scales, but accurate weights find favor with him.”
title: It’s a Thin Line Between Love and Hate
devotional: |-
  It’s a thin line between love and hate.
  
  At least that’s what the old song says.
  
  But have you ever wondered what makes that line so thin?
  
  Maybe it’s because both love and hate are deeply concerned.
  
  Neither one is indifferent.
  
  Neither one is neutral.
  
  Neither one shrugs its shoulders and walks away.
  
  And that’s what caught my attention when I read today’s proverb.
  
  Imagine an old marketplace.
  
  A merchant stands behind his table selling grain.
  
  He’s friendly.
  
  Everybody likes him.
  
  He remembers names. Tells jokes. Smiles often.
  
  But hidden beneath his scale is a tiny weight.
  
  Just enough to cheat every customer a little.
  
  Not enough to start a fight.
  
  Not enough to get caught.
  
  Just enough to slowly distort reality.
  
  One ounce here.
  
  One ounce there.
  
  And over time, trust begins to disappear.
  
  The proverb says God detests dishonest scales.
  
  That’s strong language.
  
  But I don’t think the writer is trying to tell us God has emotional mood swings.
  
  I think he’s trying to communicate something else.
  
  God is so committed to truth, fairness, and reality that He refuses to give dishonesty any place to stand.
  
  No room.
  
  No footing.
  
  No legitimacy.
  
  Because dishonest scales don’t merely affect transactions.
  
  They damage relationships.
  
  And once trust is broken, reconciliation becomes difficult.
  
  Most of us don’t own ancient scales.
  
  But we all have ways of tipping them.
  
  Telling half the story.
  
  Making ourselves look better.
  
  Leaving out important details.
  
  Presenting a version of reality that benefits us.
  
  Small distortions.
  
  Tiny weights.
  
  But enough to move the scale.
  
  Here’s the beautiful thing.
  
  God doesn’t oppose dishonesty because He’s against people.
  
  He opposes dishonesty because He’s for relationship.
  
  Truth creates trust.
  
  Trust creates connection.
  
  Connection creates unity.
  
  And unity is where love grows.
  
  Maybe that’s why dishonest scales receive such strong language.
  
  Because anything that consistently works against reconciliation will always meet resistance from a God who is constantly working toward it.
remember: |-
  God opposes what destroys trust because He desires connection.
prayer: |-
  God, remove every dishonest weight and make my scales true.
challenge: |-
  Pay attention to your conversations today.
  
  Notice any temptation to exaggerate, minimize, or distort reality.
  
  Choose honesty, even in the small things.
  
  Every truthful word helps recalibrate the scale.
