scriptureReference: Proverbs 12:3
translation: NLT
verseText: |-
  "Wickedness never brings stability, but the godly have deep roots."
title: Deep Roots
devotional: |-
  Everybody wants something that lasts.
  
  Something stable.
  
  Something secure.
  
  Something that can't be taken away.
  
  We spend a lot of our lives trying to build that kind of security.
  
  A better job.A bigger savings account.A stronger network.A nicer house.
  
  None of those things are bad.
  
  The problem is that every one of them can be lost.
  
  And that's exactly where today's proverb takes us.
  
  Notice the contrast.
  
  Wickedness promises quick results.
  
  Shortcuts.
  
  Immediate gains.
  
  Fast growth.
  
  But Proverbs says it never produces stability.
  
  Why?
  
  Because anything built on distortion eventually has to support a reality it wasn't designed to carry.
  
  Sooner or later, the foundation gives way.
  
  But godliness works differently.
  
  Godliness continually brings us back to center.
  
  Back to truth.
  
  Back to reality.
  
  Back to alignment with God.
  
  And over time that alignment becomes a root system.
  
  Deep.Hidden.Strong.
  
  Think about a musician.
  
  A truly gifted musician can lose almost everything.
  
  The guitar can be stolen.
  
  The studio can close.
  
  The record deal can disappear.
  
  The audience can move on.
  
  But if music is deeply rooted inside them, they haven't lost the source.
  
  Give them another instrument.
  
  Give them another room.
  
  Give them another opportunity.
  
  And the music will emerge again.
  
  Because the root is still there.
  
  The resources changed.
  
  The source remained.
  
  That's what this proverb is talking about.
  
  It's not promising that you'll never lose a house.
  
  Never lose a job.
  
  Never lose a relationship.
  
  It's pointing to something much deeper.
  
  A person rooted in God's wisdom possesses something that circumstances cannot remove.
  
  Because their stability doesn't come from what they have.
  
  It comes from who they are becoming.
  
  The deepest treasures in life are not possessions.
  
  They're roots.
  
  And roots remain even when storms strip away branches.
remember: |-
  What is deeply rooted within you can never be taken from you.
prayer: |-
  God, deepen my roots in Your wisdom, truth, and love.
challenge: |-
  Ask yourself this question:
  
  What in my life could survive if everything else changed?
  
  Spend a few moments strengthening that root today.
