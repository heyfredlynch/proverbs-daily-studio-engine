Proverbs 10:3

“The Lord does not let the righteous go hungry, but he thwarts the craving of the wicked.”

Today’s Episode: God Be Meddling

**Wednesday, 2026-06-10 | ESV**

Have you ever had something not work out and later realized it was one of the best things that could have happened?

The relationship ended.

The deal fell through.

The opportunity disappeared.

The door slammed shut.

And at the time all you could think was:

“Why, God?”

Then years later you look back and say:

“Thank God.”

Funny how that works.

Sometimes what feels like rejection turns out to be protection.

Sometimes what feels like disappointment turns out to be direction.

Or as we might say where I come from:

God be meddling.

The word that catches my attention is “thwarts.”

Some translations say frustrates.

Others say rejects.

Either way, God is interfering with something.

But notice carefully what He opposes.

The proverb doesn’t say God frustrates the person.

It says He frustrates the craving.

The appetite.

The desire.

The impulse.

And that’s an important distinction.

Because God isn’t out to get people.

He’s out to get people free.

Sometimes the thing standing between us and freedom is the very thing we want most.

Think about a small child reaching for an electrical outlet.

The child sees curiosity.

The parent sees danger.

The child experiences frustration.

The parent is providing protection.

Same event.

Different perspective.

Maybe that’s why some prayers go unanswered.

Maybe that’s why some doors stay closed.

Maybe that’s why some cravings never seem to work out the way we imagined.

What feels like divine resistance may actually be divine mercy.

The beautiful thing about God is that He sees further than we do.

He sees where certain roads lead.

He sees where certain habits end.

He sees where certain desires eventually take us.

And because He loves us, He sometimes steps into the path of our cravings.

Not because He enjoys frustrating us.

But because He enjoys freeing us.

The older I get, the more I realize that some of God’s greatest blessings arrived disguised as interruptions.

Some of His greatest mercies arrived disguised as closed doors.

And some of His greatest acts of love looked an awful lot like frustration in the moment.

## 🔥 Remember
God is not out to get you.
He’s out to get you free.

## 🙏🏽 Prayer
God, give me wisdom to trust Your interruptions and protection.

## 🎯 Today's Challenge
Think about one disappointment from your past that later became a blessing.

Spend a few moments reflecting on what it taught you.

Then thank God for a door that didn’t open.
